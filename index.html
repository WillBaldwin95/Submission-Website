My CARP Task!

I wanted to incorporate the "T" and "F" of the True/ False Festival in a geometric and overlapping style. Also, by structuring the two letters as I have and including the logo opposite them I'm trying to follow the rule of thirds.

In this poster I wanted to continue the shape of the logo to the whole poster by elongating the axis present in the the logo to the whole page but also contrast the logo by swapping the colours present in the logo.

This poster uses repetition of the letters in the logo but enlarged to fill their repective segments of the page. I also continues the same colours for both the background and the letters themselves.

For my final choice I used my initial poster design but with some adjustments. Elongating the second half of the "F" to make the intention clearer. Also tiding up the text and adjustiung the font.

My Type Task!

The Axial System. To avoid this example looking like a restaraunt menu, I have limited myself to only one piece of text being centred on the page and the rest being aligned to the central axis.

The Radial System. This system relies on all your text originating from or converging on a single point. Usually from the centre of a circle both on and off screen.

The Dilatational System. I enjoyed using this system to create an interesting effect. Arching text shrinking in scale can be used to demonstrate priority.

The Random System. The biggest thought in my head making this was simply; "Is it still legible?" If the answer was a clear yes then it easn't random enough. I think I got it where I wanted it.

The Grid System. This one felt tricky to do, the advice I found seemed to be along the lines of, make a grid without making a grid which seemed maddingly unhelpful but I feel I achieved what the system is aiming for.

The Transitional System. I like this system even if it is difficult to find examples of. I like the way that everything flows together to create a uniform piece of text.

The Bilateral System. At risk of sounding like a plagiarist I find this system quite dull, it feels like a cross between the grid system and the axial system and I don't much like either of those, I can see it's uses though.

The Modular System. This system is really fun to use and had I some more skills with the programs involved I think I would have had a more interesting piece to show of this system.
